About LEVIN

LEVIN is named in tribute to Dr. Gilbert V. Levin, a pioneering scientist in space biology whose experiments on NASA’s Viking missions in 1976 provided the first direct tests for life on Mars. Levin’s groundbreaking “Labeled Release” experiment reported results consistent with microbial activity, sparking decades of debate and inspiring future astrobiology missions. The name also honors the Martian crater Levin, a symbol of exploration and discovery on another world. Our logo reflects this connection, representing Mars and its landscapes, and stands as a reminder of our mission: exploring the biology of space.

Research Explorer

A Research Explorer in Biology is a platform or tool designed to help scientists, students, and enthusiasts navigate, access, and analyze a vast range of biological research data. It aggregates publications, experimental results, and datasets from various sources, allowing users to explore topics such as genetics, microbiology, physiology, ecology, and more. Advanced features may include search filters, data visualization, AI-assisted summaries, and interactive dashboards that help users

RESEARCH GAPS & OPPORTUNITIES IN SPACE BIOLOGY

PLANT GROWTH IN LUNAR/MARTIAN GRAVITY

Most experiments focus on microgravity, limited data on partial gravity environments.

OPPORTUNITY: Develop crop systems adaptable to Mars habitats.

GENETIC EXPRESSION OVER LONG MISSIONS

Short-term studies exist, but limited data on missions >1 year.

OPPORTUNITY: Study DNA repair mechanisms for future Mars colonization.

COMBINED RADIATION & MICROGRAVITY EFFECTS

Most studies isolate variables; few test combined stressors.

OPPORTUNITY: Design integrated countermeasure experiments.
